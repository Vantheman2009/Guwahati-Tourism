Religious Places

Guwahati is home to numerous religious sites of significant historical and spiritual importance. These sacred places offer visitors a glimpse into the region's rich multicultural heritage and architectural brilliance.

Temples

Kamakhya Temple

Nilachal Hill, Guwahati, Assam

A revered Shakti Peetha dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya, known for its distinctive architecture.

Learn More

The Kamakhya Temple is a major Tantric center, famous for the Ambubachi Mela festival celebrating the goddess's menstruation. Its ancient roots and unique beehive-shaped dome make it a significant pilgrimage site.

Umananda Temple

Peacock Island, Brahmaputra River

A beautiful Shiva temple on Peacock Island, offering stunning river views and accessible only by boat.

Learn More

Built in the 17th century by Ahom king Gadadhar Singha, Umananda Temple features rock-cut figures and is especially vibrant during Maha Shivratri celebrations.

Basistha Temple

Basistha, Guwahati

An ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in a serene environment where three mountain streams converge.

Learn More

Believed to be the ashram of sage Basistha from the Ramayana, this temple is surrounded by lush greenery and a waterfall, offering a peaceful retreat.

Navagraha Temple

Chitrasal Hill, Guwahati

A historic temple dedicated to the nine celestial bodies, showcasing ancient astronomical significance.

Learn More

Built during the Ahom dynasty, this temple represents the nine planets with unique stone carvings and is a center for astrological studies and rituals.

ISKCON Temple

Ulubari, Guwahati

A Krishna temple known for its peaceful ambiance and spiritual activities.

Learn More

Part of the global ISKCON network, this temple hosts daily prayers, kirtans, and festivals like Janmashtami, attracting devotees and visitors alike.

Doul Govinda Temple

North Guwahati

A famous Vaishnavite temple on the banks of the Brahmaputra River.

Learn More

Dedicated to Lord Krishna, this temple is renowned for its Holi celebrations (Doul Utsav) and offers scenic views of the river and surrounding hills.

Purva Tirupati Balaji Temple

Betkuchi, Guwahati

A beautiful temple dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, reflecting South Indian architectural styles.

Learn More

Built with white marble, this temple is a replica of Tirupati Balaji, offering a serene environment and hosting vibrant festivals like Brahmotsavam.

Lankeshwar Temple

Lankeshwar Hill, Guwahati

A less crowded but significant Shiva temple offering tranquility and scenic views.

Learn More

Located near Gauhati University, this ancient temple is carved into a hill and is a hidden gem for devotees seeking peace away from the city bustle.

Consecrated in the 1990s, it features vibrant stained glass and spacious interiors, hosting regular masses and community events.

Learn More

Founded by American missionaries, it supports a strong youth ministry and various community services, reflecting its historical growth.

An ancient mosque with historical significance and traditional Islamic architecture.

Learn More

Built during the Ahom dynasty, it blends Mughal and Assamese styles, serving as a center for worship and education.

A prominent mosque with minarets and domes, adorned with calligraphy and patterns.

Learn More

Known for Friday prayers, it includes a madrasa and serves a large Muslim community in Guwahati.

A contemporary mosque in a bustling area, serving locals and travelers alike.

Learn More

Established in the commercial district, it offers traveler facilities and hosts Ramadan iftars.

A revered Islamic pilgrimage site said to contain soil from Mecca.

Learn More

Built in the 17th century by Pir Giasuddin Auliya, this mosque atop Garurachal Hill attracts both Muslim and Hindu pilgrims, symbolizing religious harmony.
